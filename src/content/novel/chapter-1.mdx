# Breaking the Paradigm

## Chapter 1. The Awakening

***

Like all eternal tales, our story began at midnight, amidst relentless rain and a suffocating heat that made the blood boil. Adding to the unbearable weather was the guilt gnawing at me from the inside. After long and agonizing deliberation, I had finally passed judgment on myself—the decision I made today was not merely a mistake, but a crime.

I had surrendered; Cardinal Demetreus's admonition—or rather, a threat masquerading as dogma—had done its work. With my own hands, I destroyed all the evidence I had gathered myself, along with everything Poco had sent me. It was data that directly and brazenly contradicted Saint Isaac's Universal Law of Gravitation. I had to choose between truth and dogma, and I, **Lucius Cornelius**, a scientist, sought refuge in dogma.

In this sweet moment of self-destruction and humiliation, a stubborn and extremely demanding knock echoed at the door. I have no idea how or why, but I opened it anyway. The sight on the threshold was simultaneously comical and mesmerizing—the thin, wet dress clinging to Clio's perfect body could no longer serve its primary function. Her disheveled hair, heavy with rain, now gently caressed her proud neck. Only her fiercely intelligent green eyes continued their traditional work: bewitching you to the point of stripping away your ability to speak or think.

***

...And now Clio was already asleep. She lay motionless in my bed, yet her body still spoke to me, enchanting me and giving me no peace. At the sight of the sleeping Clio, even the most untalented bard would have composed his greatest poem. I looked at her and felt the pressure of her invisible, yet tangible, inner strength. This power was pushing me in the exact direction I was so terrified to take. I could have said that my awakening was driven by her indomitable spirit, but in that moment, I preferred my rebellion to be much more poetic.

When Clio rolled over in her sleep, the light catching the perfect curve of her waist, I decided it was a sign sent from the universe. Yes, I had received the universe's blessing to defy the system, and this blessing manifested in the form of Clio's ideal body. You would probably agree that there is a very solid logical connection between a naked back and the destruction of the system.

I couldn't waste another second; the battle with dogma was still ongoing, and I had to do one thing immediately—recover the deleted files. Besides, Clio's back would be much more visible from my desk. Until now, I hadn't cared at all about what was happening around my workspace. To tell you the truth, in the euphoria of making an existential decision, I hadn't fully noticed anything. As usual, I roughly grabbed my armchair, which I thought was empty.
